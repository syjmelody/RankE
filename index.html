Overview

Post-training alignment for discrete text-to-image (T2I) generation has only optimized the autoregressive (AR) policy while keeping the VQ decoder frozen, implicitly assuming it generalizes equally well to any policy output[cite: 4]. We identify a fundamental flaw in this assumption—Latent Covariate Shift: the decoder, trained solely on deterministic ground-truth tokens, degrades on the distributionally shifted tokens from stochastic policy sampling[cite: 5].

To close this gap, we introduce RankE, the first end-to-end post-training framework purpose-built for discrete T2I models[cite: 6].

Casting the problem as Generalized Expectation-Maximization, RankE alternates between a Latent Ranking step that refines the AR policy via group-relative preference optimization and a Pixel Ranking step that adapts the decoder through a composite objective of Reward-Aware Ranking GAN loss and Perception Consistency Distillation[cite: 7]. On LlamaGen-XL (775M), RankE achieves an FID of 15.21 and a CLIP score of 33.76 on MS-COCO 30K, outperforming frozen-decoder baselines and establishing a new Pareto front across fidelity, alignment, and compositional reasoning[cite: 9].

Framework

After pre-training the VQ tokenizer, existing works only optimize the AR generator with the sampled latent rollouts by the RL ranking loss (policy gradients) with the T2I rewards while freezing the decoder, which will cause the Latent Covariate Shift between the generator and the decoder[cite: 82].

RankE applies an alternative training pipeline to co-evolve both the generator and the decoder by RL objectives with the T2I rewards (for the generator & decoder) and pixel rewards (for the decoder)[cite: 83]. By unlocking the decoder, this joint optimization directly mitigates this shift, ensuring that the latent representations remain firmly grounded in high-fidelity visual outputs[cite: 84].

Results

Latent Covariate Shift & Pareto Front

We measure latent covariate shift by the KL divergence between each model's token distribution and the ground-truth VQ token distribution[cite: 46]. Standard RL further increases the divergence by 21% over SFT, whereas RankE keeps it near the SFT level via decoder co-evolution[cite: 47]. Furthermore, standard RL yields small CLIP improvements with early saturation and non-improving FID, while RankE achieves Pareto improvement by increasing CLIP and decreasing FID[cite: 49].

Qualitative Comparisons

RankE yields precise attributes and fine-grained details corresponding to the text prompts when compared to the base model[cite: 255]. While the standard RL method (GRPO) often introduces visual artifacts, RankE significantly reduces these artifacts and maintains superior image quality, demonstrating the effectiveness of adapting the decoder to policy-sampled latents[cite: 256].
